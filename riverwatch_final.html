Key Finding: Conductivity Exceedances Have Spread Watershed-Wide

In 2011, elevated conductivity was concentrated at heavily urbanized sites — Ellicott Creek (83% out of range) and Scajaquada Creek (80%). By 2025, 66% of all samples across the entire watershed exceeded the guideline. This dramatic shift likely reflects the cumulative impact of road salt and suburban development spreading beyond city cores. The new chloride monitoring program (launched 2024) is now tracking this directly — with Rush Creek and Scajaquada Creek confirmed as hotspots.

Program Evolution
Riverwatch Through the Years

From a baseline volunteer water chemistry program to a multi-parameter, watershed-wide citizen science network tracking emerging contaminants.

2011
Program launches. 28 sites · 882 individual samples · 296 volunteer hours. Core focus on 5 baseline chemistry parameters. Staff test E. coli at 3 sites.
DOpH ConductivityTurbidity TemperatureE. coli (3 sites)
2014
YELP program launched. Buffalo public school students join active water quality sampling, expanding E. coli monitoring throughout the city and integrating results with the Swim Guide platform.
YELPE. coli Expansion Swim Guide Integration
2018
WQI scoring introduced; ~95 sites monitored. Water Quality Index grades (A–F) applied to all sites. TDS added as a parameter. Microplastics pilot launched with Labatt USA — first plastic pellet tracking in the watershed.
~95 SitesWQI Scoring TDS AddedMicroplastics Pilot
2020
Nurdle Patrol begins after a large concentration of plastic pellets is discovered at Gratwick Park, North Tonawanda. Over 10,000 nurdles collected in 2025 alone.
Nurdle Patrol Plastic Pollution Monitoring
2022
PFAS surface water testing launched. "Forever chemical" sampling begins at 22 waterways in direct response to emerging public health concerns.
PFAS (22 Sites) Emerging Contaminants
2024–2025
Chloride monitoring added. Road salt impacts tracked at 5 waterways across pre-winter, peak-winter, and spring seasons. By 2025: 58 baseline sites + 31 bacteria sites + PFAS + chloride + nurdle patrol all operating simultaneously.
